# Study Protocol

## 1. Research objective

This study is a methodological demonstration using Eunomia sample OMOP data. The research
question is whether new use of Celecoxib, compared with new use of Diclofenac, is associated with
gastrointestinal hemorrhage within 30 days after treatment initiation, after prespecified
adjustment for measured baseline covariates.

The target is Celecoxib (concept ID 1118084), the active comparator is Diclofenac (concept ID
1124300), and the outcome is gastrointestinal hemorrhage (concept ID 192671). Results are not
clinical evidence, regulatory evidence, or drug safety conclusions. No result will be described
as more than an observational association, and no drug will be described as safe or unsafe.

## 2. Data source

The data source is Eunomia, a synthetic or sample dataset represented in the OMOP Common Data
Model. It is used solely to demonstrate a reproducible OHDSI study workflow. Its size, content,
and data-generating characteristics do not support clinical or regulatory inference. The dataset
and software versions used for an analysis run will be recorded with its reproducibility
artifacts.

The general feasibility analysis was generated by `scripts/02_run_feasibility.R`. The targeted
aggregate review is recorded in `results/tables/targeted_feasibility_review.csv` and was completed
before final cohort construction and outcome modeling. It reported 1,800 target subjects, 830
comparator subjects, 113 target outcomes, and 46 comparator outcomes. These are preliminary
engineering-feasibility counts, not effect estimates, and may differ after final cohort SQL
validation.

## 3. Study design

This is a retrospective, new-user, active-comparator cohort study demonstration. Celecoxib and
Diclofenac were treated as candidate NSAID alternatives for active-comparator design screening.
This treatment of the pair is a methodological design assumption, not evidence of clinical
equivalence, exchangeability, or identical indication. The pair was retained because it passed
the prespecified engineering thresholds; it was not chosen using an effect estimate, p-value, or
observed association. The configured thresholds require at least 100 subjects per arm, at least
20 outcomes in total, and at least 5 outcomes per arm.

The pair is not evidence of clinical equivalence.

The treatment pair and outcome are frozen before final cohort construction and outcome modeling.
The pair must not be changed after examining results unless the change is documented as a
protocol deviation.

## 4. Target population

The target population comprises new users of Celecoxib (concept ID 1118084) who meet all prior
observation, washout, prior outcome, and cohort-entry requirements. A person must have a first
qualifying Celecoxib exposure during an eligible observation period. Each person may contribute
one qualifying entry only.

## 5. Active comparator population

The active comparator population comprises new users of Diclofenac (concept ID 1124300) who meet
the same prior observation, washout, prior outcome, and cohort-entry requirements as the target
population. A person must have a first qualifying Diclofenac exposure during an eligible
observation period. Each person may contribute one qualifying entry only.

## 6. Index date

The index date is the date of first qualifying exposure after washout. For the target population,
this is the first qualifying Celecoxib exposure; for the comparator population, it is the first
qualifying Diclofenac exposure. Treatment assignment and eligibility are determined at index,
without using future information.

Only one qualifying entry per person is permitted.

## 7. Prior observation requirement

Eligible persons must have at least 180 continuous days inside an observation period before the
index date. The 180-day interval is evaluated before index and must not be bridged across a gap in
recorded observation.

## 8. Washout definition

A new user has no exposure to either Celecoxib or Diclofenac during the 180 days before index.
The washout applies symmetrically to both study arms. Exposure on the index date defines cohort
entry and is not part of the pre-index washout interval.

## 9. Outcome definition

The outcome is Gastrointestinal hemorrhage, anchored to OMOP concept ID 192671. The binary
primary outcome indicates whether a qualifying record of gastrointestinal hemorrhage occurs in
the primary time-at-risk window. Persons with gastrointestinal hemorrhage before or on the index
date are excluded. The final outcome SQL and concept-set implementation must be validated before
modeling and must remain consistent across study arms.

Prior gastrointestinal hemorrhage before or on index is excluded.

## 10. Time-at-risk window

The primary risk window is Day 1 through day 30 after index, inclusive. Index day is day 0 and is
not included. Follow-up is evaluated over this fixed window within observable data; any handling
of incomplete observable follow-up will be reported explicitly.

## 11. Baseline covariates

Baseline covariates will characterize demographics and recorded clinical history available in
OMOP before index, including age, sex, prior conditions, prior drug exposures, procedures, and
other available pre-index health-care utilization measures. Covariate definitions and lookback
windows will be generated reproducibly and reported with the analysis.

The covariate window is strictly before index. No index-day or post-index variables, including
future treatment, future outcomes, or future utilization, may be included in the propensity-score
model or used for adjustment.

Baseline covariates are measured strictly before index.

Index-day and post-index variables are excluded.

## 12. Primary estimand

The primary estimand uses the ATT estimand and is described as follows:

> Adjusted observational association under the stated design assumptions.

It compares the fixed-window binary outcome between Celecoxib and Diclofenac in the matched
population represented by Celecoxib initiators. The estimand is an adjusted association and must
be interpreted only as an adjusted observational association.

## 13. Propensity-score model

The propensity score will be estimated from prespecified baseline covariates measured strictly
before index. The model predicts observed initiation of Celecoxib rather than Diclofenac. Model
specification, included covariates, exclusions, warnings, and convergence diagnostics will be
retained as reproducibility artifacts. Treatment pair selection and model revision must not be
driven by outcome effect estimates or p-values.

## 14. Adjustment strategy

The primary adjustment is propensity-score matching with a 1:1 ratio, a standard-deviation
caliper, and 5 percent preference-score trimming, as configured. Matching targets the ATT
population. The configured trimming is part of the primary propensity-score specification, not
an additional sensitivity analysis. The numbers retained and excluded in each arm, the achieved
overlap, and matching diagnostics will be reported.

## 15. Balance assessment

Covariate balance will be assessed before and after adjustment using absolute standardized mean
differences. The prespecified threshold is 0.1. P-values will not be used to determine balance.
The distribution of balance diagnostics and any important residual imbalances will be reported;
passing a threshold will not be presented as proof that confounding has been eliminated.

The absolute standardized mean difference threshold is 0.1.

P-values are not used to assess covariate balance.

## 16. Outcome model

The primary outcome model is logistic regression for a binary gastrointestinal hemorrhage outcome during the fixed day 1 through day 30 risk window.
The treatment coefficient will be exponentiated and reported as an odds ratio.
Uncertainty will be estimated using a matched-set cluster-robust sandwich variance with match identifier as the clustering unit and the finite-sample CR1 correction.
A two-sided 95% Wald confidence interval will be reported with the numbers of subjects and events analyzed.
The result will be labeled as an adjusted observational association under the stated design assumptions and will not be interpreted as causal.

## 17. Subgroup analyses

Prespecified subgroup analyses are:

- sex categories available in OMOP;
- age below 65 years; and
- age 65 years or above.

Subgroups are descriptive analyses of adjusted observational associations. Categories, sample
sizes, event counts, overlap, balance, and failures of estimability will be reported. Subgroup
findings will be reported only as observational associations and will not be used to claim
clinical benefit, harm, safety, or lack of safety.

## 18. Sensitivity analyses

Prespecified sensitivity analyses will compare:

- risk windows of days 1–14, 1–30, and 1–60, each inclusive;
- propensity-score matching versus propensity-score weighting;
- washout periods of 180 and 365 days.

Sensitivity analyses will preserve the selected treatment pair and outcome. Differences across
analyses will be reported without selecting a preferred result on the basis of magnitude,
direction, statistical significance, or p-values.

## 19. Missing-data handling

Missingness will be described explicitly for variables used in eligibility, covariate
construction, adjustment, and outcome analysis. Structural absence of an OMOP record will not be
silently equated with a measured negative value unless that interpretation is part of a stated
variable definition. No imputation will be performed without a prespecified, recorded rule. Any
implemented missing-category, complete-case, or imputation approach and its assumptions will be
reported rather than left implicit.

## 20. Statistical uncertainty

Adjusted association estimates will be accompanied by confidence intervals calculated with
uncertainty estimation appropriate for the matched design. Cohort sizes, event counts, overlap,
covariate balance, retained matched sample size, and relevant model diagnostics will be reported
regardless of the direction or statistical significance of the estimate. Null, unstable, and
negative findings will not be suppressed.

The study may be reported as not estimable if overlap, balance, event counts, effective sample
size, or model convergence are inadequate. No alternative treatment pair may be substituted after
reviewing results without a documented protocol deviation.

## 21. Software and reproducibility

The analytical implementation will use R and SQL, DatabaseConnector, Eunomia, and OHDSI methods,
including CohortMethod where feasible. SQL will be rendered and translated with SqlRender. Package
dependencies will be managed with `renv`, tests with `testthat`, and reporting with Quarto.
Configuration, deterministic random seeds, executed commands, aggregate outputs, diagnostics, and
software versions will be retained so the demonstration can be reproduced without committing
database files or row-level person data.

## 22. Limitations

Eunomia is synthetic or sample demonstration data and does not represent a clinical population.
Its concepts, event frequencies, observation patterns, and sample size may not reflect routine
care. The design can be affected by residual and unmeasured confounding, exposure and outcome
misclassification, incomplete observation, limited overlap, sparse events, missing data, and
model instability. A short fixed risk window and a binary logistic model further limit the scope
of interpretation.

Meeting engineering feasibility or balance thresholds does not establish clinical validity,
exchangeability, or an interpretation beyond observational association. Results are not clinical
evidence, regulatory evidence, or drug safety conclusions. The required statistical language is:

> Adjusted observational association under the stated design assumptions.

## 23. Protocol deviations

No protocol deviations are recorded at protocol freeze. Any change to the frozen target,
comparator, outcome, eligibility rules, time-at-risk, covariates, estimand, adjustment, balance
criterion, outcome model, or sensitivity analyses will be recorded in
`docs/protocol_deviations.md`. Each record will state whether the change occurred before or after
viewing results and its expected impact.
